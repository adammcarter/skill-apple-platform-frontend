# Microcopy And Labeling

Use this reference when naming actions, controls, alerts, menus, and empty states in Apple-platform UI.

## General Rules

- Be concrete and short.
- Prefer verbs for actions and nouns for destinations or sections.
- Avoid clever wording that obscures purpose.
- Let standard Apple terminology win over brand-invented synonyms for common controls.

## Buttons And Actions

- Label actions by outcome: `Save`, `Export`, `Share`, `Try Again`, `Reveal in Finder`.
- Avoid vague labels like `Continue` unless the context is obvious.
- Use sentence case unless the platform or existing app style clearly dictates otherwise.

## Menus And Commands

- Keep menu labels scannable and conventional.
- Use ellipsis only when an action opens another step or requires more input.
- Reserve destructive wording for genuinely destructive outcomes.

## Empty States

- State what is missing.
- Explain why it matters only if the reason is not obvious.
- Offer the next action when one exists.
- Keep tone calm and useful, not theatrical.

## Alerts And Confirmation

- Title the decision clearly.
- Use body text to explain consequence or irreversibility.
- Make the primary action explicit.
- Do not present ambiguous confirmation buttons that require rereading the message.

## Labels And Section Titles

- Prefer plain language over internal terminology.
- Keep related labels parallel in structure.
- Make inspector and settings labels precise enough to stand alone.
